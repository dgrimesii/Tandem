# Tandem — Design Vision

## The Three Time Horizons

| Horizon | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Right Now** | Weight loss support during Wegovy journey |
| **This Year** | Habit and routine building for consistent fitness |
| **Long Game** | Functional longevity investment for 50s, 60s, and beyond |

---

## Design Principles

### 1. Effort over output
What gets measured and celebrated is how hard something was *for you*, not absolute performance. Effort points account for baseline fitness differences between users.

### 2. Collaborative, not competitive
Shared quests create joint ownership without scoreboard dynamics. Neither user should feel behind the other.

### 3. Consistency over intensity
The streak and quest systems reward showing up, not performing. A 15-minute walk counts. A rest day doesn't end a streak.

### 4. Always forward-looking
The app never surfaces what wasn't done. Every message, summary, and notification points toward the next opportunity.

### 5. Failure is survivable
Streak Shields, recovery windows, Journey Streaks, and dignified resets ensure one bad day — or week — never feels like the end of the story.

### 6. Private plans, shared outcomes
Individual plans and progress details are personal. Shared quest progress is visible to both users. Partner risk visibility is opt-in.

### 7. Pain is information, not excuse
Pain day logging is trusted and never questioned. It surfaces modified activity options and contributes to a medical-grade log over time.

---

## Amy-Specific Design Constraints

These are non-negotiable requirements that touch every layer of the app:

- **Never surface failure** — no broken streak icons, no "you missed your goal", no comparison to prior performance
- **Reduce activation energy** — "Just Start" button always visible; suggested next activity always pre-populated
- **Warm, specific language** — all copy reviewed against "never say" list; celebrations reference the actual activity, not generic praise
- **Streaks have stakes but survive misses** — Streak Shields and recovery windows preserve motivational power without shame spirals
- **Pain days are first-class** — knee pain and plantar fasciitis are real; pain day mode always offers a qualifying alternative
- **Wins-only weekly summary** — no gaps, no missed targets, only what was accomplished

## Language Guidelines

### Never say
- "You missed your goal"
- "You're behind this week"
- "You only completed X"
- "Don't break your streak"
- "You haven't logged today"
- Any comparison to previous performance

### Always say
- "Here's your next opportunity"
- "Every session counts toward your total"
- "You showed up — that's the work"
- "Keep your momentum going"
- "Ready to add something to your week?"
